How to use MediLens
A plain-English guide to Medicare provider intelligence — what the data means, why it matters, and how to use each feature.
Core Features
🔍 Provider Search
Search by NPI number for a specific provider, or use last name + state + city to browse. Sort results by Medicare payments, DME volume, patient count, or risk score using the sort buttons.
📊 Metric Cards
Every card on the provider screen is clickable. Click Medicare payments to see top earners in that specialty. Click DME payments to see DME rankings. Each card reveals a deeper ranked list.
🤖 AI Analysis
The AI tab lets you ask natural language questions about any provider. It reads all three datasets and explains billing patterns, flags outliers, and interprets clinical data in plain English.
📈 Overview Dashboard
See aggregate Medicare statistics — total payments, top specialties by DME volume, hospice coverage rates. Click any overview card to drill into ranked provider lists.
Key Terms Explained
NPI
National Provider Identifier — a unique 10-digit ID assigned to every Medicare provider. Think of it as a provider's Social Security number for billing purposes.
PECOS
Provider Enrollment, Chain and Ownership System — CMS's database of all enrolled Medicare providers and what they're authorized to order. If a provider isn't in PECOS, claims get denied.
DME
Durable Medical Equipment — medical devices like wheelchairs, oxygen equipment, CPAP machines, and hospital beds. Providers must be specifically authorized to order DME or the claim is denied.
Part B
Medicare coverage for outpatient services — doctor visits, lab tests, and imaging. Providers must be enrolled to refer for Part B services.
HHA
Home Health Agency referrals. When a physician certifies that a patient needs home health care, they must be authorized to do so in PECOS.
Hospice
End-of-life care certification. Since June 2024, physicians certifying hospice must be enrolled or opted out of Medicare — claims are denied otherwise.
PMD
Power Mobility Devices — motorized wheelchairs. A subset of DME with its own authorization requirements enforced since April 2014.
Risk Score
Average HCC risk score of a provider's patient panel. Higher = sicker patients. Useful for context — a high-risk provider billing more isn't necessarily suspicious.
Peer Benchmark
How a provider compares to all other providers of the same specialty. A DME payment that's +200% above peers in the same specialty may warrant closer review.
Denial Risk
When a provider lacks authorization for a service category they're ordering, CMS automatically denies the claim. MediLens flags these mismatches before submission.
Data Sources
CMS PECOS Order & Referring
Updated twice weekly by CMS. Lists all 2M+ Medicare-enrolled providers and their authorization flags. Data as of April 2026.
Medicare DME by Referring Provider
387K providers who referred at least one DME claim in 2023. Includes payment amounts, claim counts, and patient demographics. Source: CMS data.cms.gov, 2023.
Medicare Physician & Other Practitioners
1.26M providers with complete 2023 billing activity — services, payments, patient chronic conditions, and risk scores. Source: CMS data.cms.gov, 2023.
How data is joined
All three datasets are joined on NPI in DuckDB, an in-process analytical database. Specialty benchmarks are precomputed across 101 specialty groups for instant peer comparison.
